Design leadership philosophy

Great design leadership is never just one thing.

After years of leading design across global organisations, I've come to believe that the best design leaders don't just make great products — they build the conditions and culture for great products to exist, alongside great designers. That takes five distinct capabilities, held in balance at once.

01

Strategy

Setting direction with intention. Building a shared design vision and roadmap in genuine collaboration with leadership — driving meaningful change rather than reacting to daily noise.

02

Experience

Owning craft and quality. Pushing for human-centred thinking across the business and defining what "good" actually means for your organisation — then holding that bar.

03

Systems

Designing beyond the screen. Building components, patterns, and design languages that scale — so consistency becomes the default, not the exception, across every surface and team.

04

People

Leading with care. Hiring with intention, fostering a design culture where people do their best work, giving feedback that actually lands, and always developing the next generation of design leaders.

05

AI-native thinking

Designing for the AI era — not just with AI tools. Understanding how intelligence changes the interaction model, building trust into every decision point, and ensuring humans stay meaningfully in control.
