Design KPI Tree

Connect design work to outcomes that matter

Most design teams are great at shipping. Few are great at proving what it changed. This tool maps business objectives → design initiatives → UX metrics, so the impact of design is visible — not assumed.

How it works

01

Start with the business objective

Every design decision should be traceable to something the business cares about.

02

Name the design initiatives

These are the design bets — specific UX improvements you believe will move the objective.

03

Choose the right metrics

Don't start with metrics — start with user needs, then find what proves the need was met.

04

Track and report visibly

Design impact belongs in the same rooms where product and engineering report their numbers.

How it works

01

Start with the business objective

Every design decision should be traceable to something the business cares about. If you can't draw that line, you're designing without a brief.

02

Name the design initiatives

These are the design bets — specific UX improvements you believe will move the objective. Make them concrete enough to be testable.

03

Choose the right metrics

Don't start with metrics — start with user needs. Then find the metric that confirms the need was met. UMUX-Lite, SUS, CES, TFV all have specific jobs.

04

Track and report visibly

Design impact shouldn't live in a Notion doc. It belongs in the same rooms where product and engineering report their numbers.
