Compliance with Simplicity: Macquarie Bank Risk Control

In a regulated investment bank, compliance isn't optional — but a process that takes 7 days to complete is a process that gets worked around. This case study is about redesigning Macquarie's technology risk workflow so that rigour and speed stopped being opposites. The goal wasn't to simplify compliance. It was to make doing it correctly the path of least resistance — for 6,000+ engineers and 4,000+ non-technical staff across all levels of risk, operations, and governance, operating under some of the most demanding regulatory standards in financial services.

Background

Where compliance meets engineering velocity

Macquarie is well-known for its rigorous security compliance standards. Risk Control is a critical phase in Macquarie's technology risk process — the stage where teams identify, assess, and remediate technology risks before they become release blockers or regulatory issues.

The challenge: make this process faster and clearer, without compromising the rigour that makes Macquarie's security posture strong.

Underlying insight
"The system was designed to report risk — not resolve it."
Every symptom traced back to the same root cause: the interface surfaced compliance data as information to be read, not as actions to be taken. Engineers knew what the system said. They didn't know what to do next. Redesigning around resolution — not reporting — became the strategic direction for both design concepts.

Discovery

How might we streamline remediation without compromising standards?

The design challenge was precise: "How might we create a seamless experience for all scorecard remediation stages by retrofitting current fix requirements, without compromising existing security or regulatory standards?"

Through user research and interviews, iterative ideation with cross-functional teams, and high-fidelity prototyping cycles, two major design opportunities emerged.

Design Concept 01

An accessible, meaningful status system

The gap: The existing color-based status system was fundamentally broken. Research showed only 50% of users correctly interpreted the color coding — a critical failure for a compliance system where misreading a status could have serious consequences.

The solution: Replaced ambiguous colors with clear semantic labels ("Overdue", "At Risk", "On Track"), supported by iconography and consistent terminology throughout. Every status now communicates its meaning independently of color, ensuring WCAG-compliant accessibility and faster comprehension.

  • Semantic labels replace color-only coding
  • Consistent iconography reinforces status meaning
  • WCAG-compliant contrast throughout
  • Contextual tooltips explain status implications
  • Reduced misinterpretation from 50% error rate

Design Concept 02

Time as the primary signal

The gap: 70% of users couldn't identify their most urgent scorecards from the existing dense table layout. Scanning fatigue was real — engineers spent more time reading the table than actually remediating risks.

The solution: Introduced a weekly calendar view that makes time the primary organisational dimension. Risk urgency is immediately visible from the temporal layout. Action-focused detail views and integrated guidance contextualise each item at the moment of remediation.

  • Weekly calendar view surfaces deadline urgency at a glance
  • Information segmentation reduces visual noise
  • Action-focused details panel reduces context-switching
  • Integrated guidance reduces "five people" problem
  • Stand-up meetings become more efficient

Before: Dense table — status colours, no context, no action guidance

After: Calendar view with action panel — time as the primary signal, guidance at point of remediation
